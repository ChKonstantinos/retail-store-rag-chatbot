Document Title: Returns and Refunds Policy

Purpose:
This policy explains how store employees should handle customer returns, refunds, and exchanges.

Standard Return Procedure:
Customers may return products within 14 days of purchase if they provide a valid receipt.
The product must be unused, unopened, and in resalable condition.
The employee must verify the receipt, check the product condition, and confirm that the return window has not expired.

Returns Without Receipt:
If a customer does not have a receipt, the employee should first try to locate the transaction using the customer's phone number, loyalty card, or payment method.
If the transaction cannot be verified, cash refund is not allowed.
The store manager may approve store credit only if the product is clearly part of the store's assortment and is in excellent condition.

Damaged Products:
If a customer returns a damaged product, the employee must identify whether the damage was present at the time of purchase or caused after purchase.
Products damaged by customer misuse are not eligible for refund.
If the damage is confirmed as a supplier or store issue, the employee may proceed with replacement or refund.

Escalation:
Any conflict with the customer must be escalated to the store manager.
Employees should remain polite, avoid arguments, and follow the official policy.
