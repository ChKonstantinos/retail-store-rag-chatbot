Document Title: Inventory Receiving and Replenishment SOP

Purpose:
This SOP explains how employees should receive stock, check deliveries, and replenish store shelves.

Receiving Goods:
When goods arrive, the employee must compare the delivery note with the physical products.
The employee must check quantities, product codes, expiration dates, and visible damages.
Any difference between delivered quantity and delivery note must be recorded immediately.

Damaged or Missing Items:
Damaged items identified during stock receiving must be separated from sellable stock before they are placed on shelves.
Missing items must be reported to the supplier or warehouse team before the delivery is accepted in the inventory system.
The store manager must approve any adjustment in the inventory system.
This procedure applies only to goods receiving and warehouse stock control, not to customer returns.

Shelf Replenishment:
Employees should replenish shelves based on product availability, expiry dates, and sales priority.
Products with earlier expiration dates must be placed in front.
Empty shelves must be reported during the daily store walk.

Stock Accuracy:
Inventory records must be updated after receiving goods.
Employees must avoid moving products without updating the system.
Stock discrepancies must be investigated before placing new orders.
